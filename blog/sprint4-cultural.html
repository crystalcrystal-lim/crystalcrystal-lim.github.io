Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity and its benefits

Neuroplasticity refers to the brains ability to adapt, it can create new neural connections and strengthen them. Just like any muscle in the body it can be exercised and built stronger.

Possessing this understanding is beneficial as it validates the idea that there is equality in abilities. With practise, anyone is capable of learning. This comprehension encourages perseverance, consistency and confidence to strive for goals.

Engaging with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

Activity repetition and regular reviews will be beneficial for my own learning journey. Continued practise and failed attempts at Javascript will only strengthen the pathways in my brain. Reflection writing has also helped me track my learning progress and evaluate my thinking behaviour.

Increasing neuroplasticity

  • Repetition of activities
  • Put information into context and tap into already existing pathways
  • Rewards for effort
  • Enriched environments
  • Mindfulness
  • Sufficient sleep

Growth mindset and relevancy

A growth mindset is a mindset that can be improved through practise. It views failures as opportunities to learn and celebrate the process! It is incredibly important as it impacts every aspect of life. With a growth mindset one is more likely to embrace challenges, whereas one with a fixed mindset is convinced that each individual has a fixed talent, making them more likely to go for the easier solutions.

It is especially relevant in my pursuit of coding as I have entered into a completely new field. The mindset that I have throughout the journey will and has established my level of success.

Intergration of a growth mindset in my learning journey

I am at a stage where the learning content is becoming increasingly difficult and I can feel my fixed mindset creeping back in. However if I step back and appreciate the amount of knowledge I have gained during the past three weeks, I can envision how much more I can gain in the next two weeks. Consistently reflecting on my progress and celebrating my growth is vital to keep me going.

There is nothing quite like that dopamine hit when a piece of code is correct. However, I must remind myself to view mistakes and road blocks as learning opportunities that will eventuate to good code.

Continuing regular and honest reflections each day/each sprint/each end of sprint will help me to comprehend my own thinking patterns and will allow me to point out the aspects that I will need to alter.
